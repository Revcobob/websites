Need to talk to someone right now?

The Alzheimer's Association 24/7 Helpline is staffed day and night by trained, master's-level clinicians. They handle crisis calls, referrals to local community programs, dementia-related education, and emotional support — in your preferred language through their interpreter service.

Local & Regional

Help that's close to home.

East Texas-based and Texas-wide organizations with caregiver programs, in-person support groups, and referrals. Call them first — they know the local landscape.

Local · Tyler

Alzheimer's Alliance of Smith County

Tyler-based nonprofit providing caregiver education, support groups, respite resources, and family consultations across the East Texas region.

State · Texas

Texas Health and Human Services

State-level access to long-term care benefits, the Aging and Disability Resource Center, Area Agencies on Aging, and Medicaid programs that may apply to your family.

National Organizations

The trusted nationwide network.

Decades of dementia research, advocacy, and family support — built and stewarded by these national organizations.

National

Alzheimer's Association

The country's leading voluntary health organization for Alzheimer's care, support, and research. Programs and support services connect you with peers and professionals to make the plans and adjustments necessary for the road ahead.

800.272.3900 Visit website
National

BrightFocus Foundation

Funds groundbreaking research toward cures for Alzheimer's disease, macular degeneration, and glaucoma, and publishes expert family-facing information on each.

Visit website
National

The Hearthstone Institute

I'm Still Here® — the philosophy and practice behind Hearthstone's nationally recognized approach to dementia care. Grounded in the belief that people living with dementia remain whole, present, and able to grow.
