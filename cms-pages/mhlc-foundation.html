Charter

Our purpose.

Drawn verbatim from the Foundation's Restated Certificate of Formation, approved by the Texas Secretary of State on September 2, 2022.

"The Foundation is and shall be operated exclusively for charitable, scientific, religious, and educational purposes within the meaning of section 501(c)(3) of the United States Internal Revenue Code or corresponding section of any future federal tax code."

Restated Certificate of Formation, September 2, 2022.

Today, the Foundation is working on plans and designs to construct the Memory Health Life Center: a regional resource open to all educational levels including national research, focused on the development of brain health care, education, diagnostics, treatment, and quality of life, not only for the center's residents but for all those that these services will touch.

History

From 1989 to today.

Four moments in the Foundation's story so far.

  1. 1989

    Founded

    The Wood County Health Care Foundation, Inc. is formed as a 501(c)(3) by the Wood County Central Hospital District.

  2. 2013

    $250,000 gift

    A $250,000 donation supports the construction of the Quitman hospital, leased to East Texas Medical Center, today UT Health Quitman.

  3. 2022

    Re-activated

    After a period of inactivity, the Foundation files a Restated Certificate of Formation, approved by the Texas Secretary of State on September 2.

  4. Today

    Building the MHLC

    The Foundation is stewarding plans, design, partnerships, and capital for the Memory Health Life Center in Quitman.
