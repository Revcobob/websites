Industries we serve

Eight Texas conversations we know how to walk into.

A short description of each industry, written in the language the people in that industry actually use. If we are not the right firm for your sector, we will tell you so on the first call.

Health Care

Managed care, hospital systems, physician groups, dental, and behavioral health. Medicaid, managed-care contracts, scope-of-practice fights, network adequacy, HHSC posture, and the appropriations work that surrounds all of it.

Local Government & Special Districts

Cities, counties, MUDs, water districts, hospital districts, ESDs, and transit authorities. Preemption defense, bracket bills, statutory fixes, appropriations support, and the testimony preparation that lets a mayor or GM walk into a hearing ready.

Infrastructure, Water & Energy

Capital appropriations, permitting, rate-case posture, and regulator engagement. Transportation, water supply, broadband, electric generation and transmission, and the PUC, TCEQ, and Railroad Commission landscapes that decide what gets built.

Real Estate & Land Use

Property rights, development policy, statewide housing debates, appraisal reform, and association-led advocacy on the issues that move every two years.

Gaming & Regulated Industries

High-visibility issues where vote counts depend on member relationships and where coverage depends on disciplined communications. The Texas debate on regulated gaming is not a slogan war — it is a long, careful, multi-session conversation.

Associations & Coalitions

Statewide trade associations whose members watch every interim charge, every committee assignment, and every appropriations rider — and whose boards need to see results between sessions as well as during them.

Nonprofits & Foundations

Appropriations riders, interim work, and the kind of representation that fits a mission-driven budget. We work on a basis that respects how a nonprofit board has to think about every dollar.

National & Out-of-State Clients

Your Texas desk. We serve as the Capitol-facing team for national government-affairs operations whose Texas exposure has reached the point where it needs to be staffed properly.
