About the Firm

A boutique firm built around procedural mastery and quiet results.

The Haney Group exists because the Texas Capitol rewards a small number of disciplines: knowing the process, knowing the budget, knowing the people, and knowing how to talk about all three. The firm was built to keep those four disciplines in one room.

The Haney Group is a boutique Texas government affairs firm based in Austin. The firm represents statewide associations, regulated companies, local governments, health care organizations, and nonprofits at the Texas Legislature and across the executive agencies that interpret what the Legislature passes.

The firm was founded around a deliberate combination. Robert Haney spent more than twenty-five years inside the Texas House of Representatives, the last five sessions as Chief Clerk under five Speakers. Julie Haney built two decades of appropriations, communications, and chief-of-staff experience on the legislative side. The combination is purposeful: the firm pairs procedural and budgetary fluency with the message discipline that decides how a bill is received, covered, and remembered.

Clients hire The Haney Group for hard issues, close votes, and consequential budgets. We are not a volume shop. We work a deliberately small book of business so that every engagement gets a principal's attention from the first call to the last conference report.

How we work

We do not start with a tactic. We start with the question every legislator asks about your issue: who is for this, who is against it, where does it live, and what does it cost. Until those four questions have honest answers, no strategy can be trusted.

We tell clients the truth. If your priority cannot be moved this session, we will say so — and tell you what the interim work looks like to give it a chance next time. If your bill is in trouble, we will tell you what it will take to save it, even if the answer is uncomfortable.

We work as principals. The people you meet on the first call are the people who do your work. Robert and Julie are personally on every engagement.

The Principals

Two careers, one Capitol.

Austin · Texas State Capitol

Robert Haney

Principal · Founder

Robert Haney worked inside the Texas House of Representatives for more than twenty-five years. The last five sessions, he served as Chief Clerk of the Texas House, appointed by five different Speakers across both parties — a kind of bipartisan trust earned only through demonstrated competence on the floor.

As Chief Clerk, Robert managed the procedural backbone of every bill the House considered: calendars, certifications, journals, technology, and the rules under which Members debated. He led the modernization of House operations, including the transition to electronic bill filing — a project that retired a century-old paper process and saved the state over three hundred thousand dollars per session.

Nationally, Robert served as President of the American Society of Legislative Clerks and Secretaries (ASLCS), the professional society of legislative procedural officers across the United States. He continues to advise chambers nationwide on modernization and parliamentary procedure. Texas Capitol Inside named him a Rising Star in 2023.

"Most of what looks like influence in a session is actually preparation done six months earlier. Our job is to be six months earlier than the people across the table."

Julie Haney

Principal

Julie Haney built her two-decade Texas career where the work is hardest to see. She served inside the office of the Chair of House Appropriations during the years that defined how Texas writes its budget today, then as Chief of Staff to a state legislator — the role that turns a member's instincts into a working political and legislative operation.

Outside the Capitol, Julie's communications work has supported clients across health care, infrastructure, and statewide associations through some of their most consequential public moments. She is the strategist clients keep on the phone when the question is what to say, when to say it, and to whom.

Her appropriations work sits behind every budget engagement the firm takes. Her communications work sits behind every press cycle a client wades into. She is, by design, the firm's bridge between the Capitol and the world the Capitol writes laws for.

"A board, a member, and a reporter need the same through-line, told in three different rooms. That's not a press kit. That's strategy."
