Appropriations & Budget Strategy.

In Texas, the budget is the bill that becomes every other bill. Our appropriations work is led by Julie Haney's two-decade record inside House Appropriations and on the legislative side of state spending decisions.

In practice

Article and rider strategy

Writing language that survives subcommittee, full committee, floor, and conference. We work the substance of the rider, the politics of the article, and the calendar of the bill — none of which can be addressed by the others alone.

LBB engagement

Working with Legislative Budget Board staff on the technical posture that determines whether a priority lives. Most of the consequential decisions in a budget are technical before they are political.

Agency coordination

Aligning client priorities with the agency's exceptional item requests and base budget posture. A rider that contradicts the agency's own request is a rider that does not survive conference.

Interim appropriations hearings

Showing up where the next budget is actually being written. Most appropriations decisions are made twelve to eighteen months before a session gavels in.

Communications around the budget

Translating Article II, III, and V into language a board can read and a member can defend. The budget is not a vocabulary test; we make it speak.

Who hires us for this

  • Health care systems and managed-care organizations.
  • Higher education partners and special funds.
  • Nonprofits, foundations, and mission-driven organizations.
  • Infrastructure clients with capital appropriations exposure.
  • Any organization whose annual plan depends on a Texas line item or rider.
