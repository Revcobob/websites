Public Affairs & Communications.

The same week, you may need to brief a member, a board chair, and a reporter on the same issue. The through-line cannot drift. Our public affairs and communications work is built around that discipline.

In practice

Narrative development

Constructing and stress-testing the client's positioning before anyone needs to use it. We do not write talking points that fall apart in the first hostile follow-up; we write the position that survives one.

Message architecture

Member talking points, board memos, press lines, and one-pagers — drafted to a single, defensible standard. Every audience hears a version of the same line; no audience hears a version that contradicts another.

Legislature 101

Half-day and full-day trainings for boards, executives, and association members on how the Texas Legislature actually works. By the end, your board can read a calendar, understand a referral, and tell a hearing from a workgroup.

Press preparation

On-the-record, off-the-record, background — and the judgment to know which conversation you are in. Reporters are not the enemy and they are not the friend; they are the third audience in every room.

Crisis posture

The first forty-eight hours of an unfavorable cycle, with a plan instead of a panic. Pre-built playbooks for the issues your organization can already see on the horizon.

Who hires us for this

  • Trade associations whose boards need to be educated as well as represented.
  • Clients with national press exposure on Texas issues.
  • Companies whose Texas footprint has become a story.
  • Coalitions that need one voice instead of seven.
